Introduction

Japanese (日本語, Nihongo) is the national language of Japan.
The writing system combines Chinese characters, known as Kanji (漢字), with two unique syllabaries derived by the Japanese from the more complex Chinese characters: hiragana (ひらがな) and katakana (カタカナ).

Final-year students of highschool are expected to know at least 2,136 "general-use" kanji. This gives you the revised government-mandated minimum literacy threshold. At the same time, this requirement is expected to push one's comprehension rate to ~99%.

However, it should be noted that most kanji have multiple ways of being read, and can make different compound meanings depending on how they are combined. For example: flower + fire = fireworks (花 + 火 = 花火), whereas fire + flower = spark (火 + 花 = 火花). In other words, simply recognizing kanji is simply the first step.

Example Compounds

Now that we've had a small intro, let us consider other fascinating compounds below:

  • 天 + 気 = 天気
    heaven + spirit = weather
  • 電 + 子 = 電子
    electricity + child = electron
  • 火 + 山 = 火山
    fire + mountain = volcano
  • 安 + 全 = 安全
    relax + complete = safety
  • 助 + 言 = 助言
    help + say = advice
  • 未 + 決 = 未決
    not yet + decide = pending
